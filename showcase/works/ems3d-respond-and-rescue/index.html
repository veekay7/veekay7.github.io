triage, treatment and evacuation in multiple situations.

About

EMS3D is an ambitious and potentially high-impact project involving Singapor's emergency medical services, utilising hospital and emergency medical datasets, and modelling patient events and optimizing patient care activities. The EMS3D is part of the Virtual Singapore project developed at Nanyang Technological Institute and developed in collaboration with Singapore General Hospital and Singapore Self Defense Forces.

I developed the simulator in a course of one year while working with a team of 2 people.

The simulator was developed based on the operating procedures of Singapore's emergency medical service system The system follows the operation flow closely starting from triage, first aid and evacuation. The style of the simulation follows a war game style. The Commander first sets up a Situation, which can take place in any location on the map, and a scenario (e.g. aftermath of explosion, terrorism etc.)

Then, the Commander can then set up regions on the map where victims would be, First Aid Points and Evacuation Points. The condition of the victims are generated at random and they are dependent on the scenario set up by the Commander.

The scene is then joined by the human players or A.I controlled players (depending on how many personnel are required). Each player has their own role in the simulation. Players can either be a Triage Officer, a First Aid Point Officer or an Evacuation Officer. The actions that players can take are limited to their role.

At the end of the simulation, each player is shown a report of their performance. Players are scored based on a proprietary algorithm we developed. The Commander also receives a report of the mission at the end of the simulation, which can be used to identify weaknesses and create new strategies for future missions.
